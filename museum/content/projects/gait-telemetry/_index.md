---
title: Gait Telemetry
summary: Open, low-cost wearable that links body motion to symptom tags. Measure motion, let the user mark exactly when something feels wrong, then correlate the two.
status: research
started: 2026-02
---

The first concrete use case is knee rehab — capturing IMU data during exercise and letting the user flag the moment pain spikes. The broader concept is motion-linked symptom logging for any body region where pain ties to load, repetition, or fatigue.

The interesting question isn't the hardware (off-the-shelf IMU + microcontroller). It's whether the user-facing tagging UX is fast enough to be honest: by the time you log a symptom retroactively, you've already lost the motion context.
